SportVIM: Managing Visual Information with Temporal and Visibility Awareness for Multi-Object Tracking in Sports Broadcasting

Maintaining identity consistency in sports broadcasts is challenging due to occlusions and irregular motion caused by random camera shifts. We propose SportVIM, a temporal- and visibility-aware framework with three modules: feature-guided association, temporal-aware fusion, and visibility-aware refinement. On the SportsMOT benchmark, SportVIM achieves 83.3% HOTA, 96.9% MOTA, 88.9% IDF1, and 77.6% AssA, demonstrating state-of-the-art robustness.

Case studies:
(Demo 1) Basketball with heavy occlusion: When ID 1 is partially blocked by another player, traditional tracking produces mismatches. SportVIM leverages joint visual and positional cues to maintain correct identity links.
(Demo 2) Soccer with rapid camera movement: During sudden pans, players such as IDs 6, 17, and 9 leave and re-enter the frame. SportVIM adapts to outdated features and preserves their identities upon return.
(Demo 3) Volleyball with severe overlap: Multiple players (IDs 4, 5, and 6) become indistinguishable when clustered together. SportVIM adjusts feature updates based on visibility, preventing drift and ensuring stable recognition.
